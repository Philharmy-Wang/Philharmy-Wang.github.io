## Current Postdoctoral Focus

My current work develops computer vision methods for non-human primate behavior understanding, with emphasis on macaque visual perception, individual identification, pose estimation, behavior analysis, and long-term multi-camera cage monitoring.

<div class="research-grid">
  <div class="research-card"><strong>Non-human Primate Visual Perception</strong><br>Visual representation and recognition methods for primate observation.</div>
  <div class="research-card"><strong>Macaque Detection and Individual Identification</strong><br>Detection and identity-aware recognition in real monitoring environments.</div>
  <div class="research-card"><strong>Pose Estimation and Behavior Understanding</strong><br>Pose-driven behavior analysis for long-term, fine-grained observation.</div>
  <div class="research-card"><strong>Long-term Multi-camera Cage Monitoring</strong><br>Multi-view monitoring pipelines for continuous animal behavior recording.</div>
  <div class="research-card"><strong>Lightweight Object Detection and Edge Deployment</strong><br>Efficient models for real-time and resource-constrained deployment.</div>
  <div class="research-card"><strong>Multimodal Remote Sensing and Wildfire Detection</strong><br>Computer vision and multimodal methods for remote sensing wildfire detection.</div>
</div>
